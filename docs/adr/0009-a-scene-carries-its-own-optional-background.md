# A Scene carries its own optional background

A **Scene** may declare a background of its own: an optional `background?: Fill` on the Scene IR, produced by the Sketch's `generate` and painted by every Scene Renderer over the **whole output surface — letterbox included** — below every Primitive. `createScene` takes it as an optional second argument; a Scene that declares none carries no field at all and renders exactly as before.

The context is issue #92 and ADR-0004's second amendment, which made the backdrop a **caller-side Render Setting**: a `background` parameter on `drawSceneFitted` / `renderToSVG` with a safe opaque-white default, deliberately *not* Scene IR ("this deliberately does NOT introduce a Scene-authored background layer — out of scope"). That was the right call for what a backdrop was then — a safety net no Sketch had to think about. It stops being right the moment a Sketch's background is **part of the composition**: leaf-field's `backgroundColor` knob colors the field's ground, and its occluder discs read as implied spheres precisely *because* their fill can match it. An image-bearing background must live on the ADR-0002 determinism spine — produced by `generate(params, seed, t)` from a param, captured by a **Preset** via the params that feed it, identical in every consumer — none of which a per-caller Render Setting can promise. We considered keeping it caller-side and having the studio thread a param into the render call: rejected because every consumer (studio, Remotion, SVG export) would need its own param→setting plumbing, the Preset would reproduce the frame only for consumers that ran it, and "what you see == what you get" would again rest on coincidence rather than structure — the exact failure #92 fixed. We also considered a full-surface background *Primitive*: rejected because a Primitive is geometry in Scene space and cannot cover the letterbox, which is part of the output surface.

**Precedence: the Scene wins.** Both renderers resolve `scene.background?.color ?? background`. A Scene-declared background is image content, so the caller's `background` parameter cannot override it, for the same reason a caller cannot recolor a Primitive. The parameter remains exactly what #92 made it — the fallback Render Setting for the common Scene that declares nothing — so no existing caller changes.

## Consequences

- The Scene IR grows one optional field and nothing else: no geometry, draw-order, or Primitive change, and a background-less Scene is byte-identical to a pre-0009 one (`createScene` omits the field rather than writing `undefined`). The video parity tests pass unchanged because their scenes declare no background.
- Resolution lives in the two renderers (`drawSceneFitted`, `renderToSVG`), not in callers, so the precedence is structural across every consumer — the same one-function-not-two-lookalikes argument as ADR-0004's fit amendment.
- The resolved color feeds the existing paint/clear logic unchanged: a Scene declaring `{ color: 'transparent' }` clears/omits-the-rect exactly as the parameter form does.
- ADR-0004's "no Scene-authored background layer" scope note is superseded by this ADR; its caller/core boundary and the Render-Setting role of the `background` parameter stand.
- A background is a single `Fill` for the whole surface. Gradients, textures, or per-region grounds are geometry (Primitives) or future `Fill` widenings — not more Scene fields.
